The market for Bullshit Science

In a typical market for consumer goods, money flows in one direction; goods flow in the other. Market for scholarly journals is different. A scholar pours her heart and soul into writing a scholarly paper. She provides her work to an academic publisher, which collects a number of such articles and bundles together as journal issue. The publisher charges libraries to subscribe, but does’nt pass any of the revenue on to the authors. Nothing inherently wrong with the idea of charging authors to publish their work. Open access publishing facilitates an increased readership which is good thing. This is story of half-glass full. There is an empty half glass as well.
Open access publishing creates a niche for low-quality journals that do not impose adequate standards. Many care for reputation and publish high quality journals but most will publish so long their check is cleared. Here is Goodhart’s law again: What a measure becomes a target, it ceased to be a good measure. Parasites on the scientific publishing system, sucking tens of million of dollars from the academic system and polluting the literature with millions of unreliable articles. Calling Bullshit
Calling Bullshit is a performative utterance in which one repudiates something objectionable. The scope of targets is broader than bullshit alone. You can call bullshit on bullshit, but you can also call bullshit on lies, treachery, trickery, or injustice.
