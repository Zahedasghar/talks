One-Day Workshop: AI for Teachers, Researchers, and Students

Author

Zahid Asghar

Published

September 24, 2024

Workshop Overview

Are you an educator, researcher, student, or data analyst in the fields of economics, education, business data and social sciences? Do you want to harness the transformative power of Artificial Intelligence (AI) to enhance your teaching, research, and learning experiences? Join us for a dynamic and insightful one-day workshop led by Zahid Asghar, an expert in data analytics, applied econometrics and getting help from AI in research and teaching.

What You’ll Gain

  • In-Depth Understanding: Learn about AI, Machine Learning (ML), Natural Language Processing (NLP), and Generative AI, and how they apply for research, teaching and as personal learning.
  • Practical Skills: Engage in hands-on sessions to familiarize yourself with AI tools for writing, data analysis, and visualization.
  • Ethical Insights: Discuss the ethical implications and challenges of integrating AI into academia.
  • Networking Opportunities: Connect with professionals and peers who are passionate about the future of education and AI.

Session 1: Demystifying AI for Educators and Learners

Time: 9:00 AM - 11:15 AM

Objectives:

  • Provide a clear understanding of AI and its core components.
  • Explore the specific applications of AI in teaching, research, and learning within economics and social sciences.
  • Discuss the ethical considerations and societal impacts of AI.

Agenda

  • 9:00 AM - 9:15 AM | Welcome and Introduction
    • Opening remarks by Zahid Asghar.
    • Overview of the workshop goals and the day’s schedule.
  • 9:15 AM - 9:45 AM | Understanding AI: From Basics to Advanced Concepts
    • What is AI?
      • Definition and key concepts.
      • Historical evolution and future trends.
    • Machine Learning (ML)
      • How machines learn from data.
      • Types: Supervised, Unsupervised, and Reinforcement Learning.
    • Natural Language Processing (NLP)
      • Interaction between computers and human language.
      • Applications in education and research.
  • 9:45 AM - 10:15 AM | Generative AI and Its Educational Applications
    • Generative AI Explained
      • How it creates new content: text, images, music.
      • Tools like GPT-4 and DALL-E.
    • Traditional AI vs. Generative AI
      • Comparative analysis.
      • Practical examples in academia.
  • 10:15 AM - 10:45 AM | The Ethical Landscape of AI in Education
    • Opportunities and Dangers
      • Benefits: Unlocking new knowledge, personalized learning.
      • Risks: Bias, privacy concerns, and misinformation.
    • The Containment Challenge
      • Managing the exponential growth of AI technology.
      • Strategies for responsible use.
  • 10:45 AM - 11:15 AM | Interactive Discussion and Q&A
    • Open floor for participants to share thoughts and ask questions.
    • Topics: AI’s impact on teaching roles, policy integrity, and learning dynamics.

Coffee Break: 11:15 AM - 11:30 AM

Session 2: Harnessing AI for Academic Writing and Research

Time: 11:30 AM - 1:00 PM

Objectives:

  • Introduce AI tools that assist in academic writing and idea generation.
  • Demonstrate how AI can improve productivity while maintaining academic integrity.
  • Provide hands-on experience with AI-driven writing assistance.

Agenda

  • 11:30 AM - 12:00 PM | AI Tools for Writing and Research
    • Overview of platforms like Grammarly and ChatGPT.
    • How AI aids in drafting, editing, and refining academic work.
  • 12:00 PM - 12:30 PM | Hands-On Activity: Collaborative Writing with AI
    • Participants draft a brief essay or research abstract using AI tools.
    • Emphasis on maintaining originality and ethical considerations.
  • 12:30 PM - 1:00 PM | Enhancing Critical Thinking with AI
    • Using AI for brainstorming and idea expansion.
    • Strategies to avoid over-reliance on AI suggestions.

Lunch Break: 1:00 PM - 2:00 PM

About the Presenter

Zahid Asghar

Zahid Asghar is a seasoned educator and AI enthusiast with a passion for integrating technology into teaching and research. With a background in economics and data analysis/analytics, Zahid has been at the forefront of exploring AI’s potential in academia. He has conducted numerous workshops and training sessions on AI for educators and researchers, focusing on practical applications and ethical considerations. Zahid’s engaging and interactive teaching style has earned him recognition as a thought leader in the field of AI in education.
